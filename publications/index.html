2025

  1. arXiv
    Constraints on the Polarization Angle Oscillations of the Crab Nebula with the Simons Array and Its Applications to the Search for Axion-Like Particles
    T. Adkins, S. S. Arani, K. Arnold, and 26 more authors
    arXiv preprint, 2025
    Abs arXiv HTML

    We present constraints on the polarization angle oscillations of the Crab Nebula observed at millimeter wavelengths with the Simons Array. We find no evidence of global polarization oscillation signals and establish upper bounds on the oscillation amplitudes and the corresponding constraints on axion-like particle (ALP) coupling constants, extending the ALP parameter space probed by CMB experiments.

  2. ApJ
    A Measurement of Atmospheric Circular Polarization with POLARBEAR
    T. Fujino, S. Takakura, S. S. Arani, and 20 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2025
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    At millimeter wavelengths, the atmospheric emission is circularly polarized owing to the Zeeman splitting of molecular oxygen by the Earth’s magnetic field. We report measurements of atmospheric circular polarization using three years of observational data from the POLARBEAR experiment at 150 GHz, employing a continuously rotating half-wave plate to modulate and detect the circular polarization signal.

2024

  1. PRD
    Exploration of the Polarization Angle Variability of the Crab Nebula with POLARBEAR and Its Application to the Search for Axion-Like Particles
    S. Adachi, T. Adkins, C. Baccigalupi, and 23 more authors
    Physical Review D, 2024
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We examine the polarization angle variability of the Crab Nebula using POLARBEAR millimeter-wave observations and apply these measurements to search for axion-like particles (ALPs). We measure the median 95% upper bound on the polarization oscillation amplitude and derive competitive constraints on the ALP-photon coupling constant as a function of ALP mass, demonstrating the power of bright astrophysical calibration sources as ALP search targets.

  2. arXiv
    Calibration of Detector Time Constant with a Thermal Source for the POLARBEAR-2A CMB Polarization Experiment
    S. Takatori, M. Hasegawa, M. Hazumi, and 22 more authors
    arXiv preprint, 2024
    Abs arXiv HTML

    The POLARBEAR-2A (PB-2A) receiver in the Simons Array requires sub-millisecond calibration of bolometer time constants to avoid biasing the polarization signal. We present a new calibrator system using a thermal source to measure and calibrate the time constants of the PB-2A transition edge sensor bolometers, and demonstrate the performance of this system during CMB observations.

  3. JATIS
    Design and Performance of a Gain Calibration System for the POLARBEAR-2a Receiver System at the Simons Array Cosmic Microwave Background Experiment
    D. Kaneko, S. Takatori, M. Hasegawa, and 11 more authors
    Journal of Astronomical Telescopes, Instruments, and Systems, 2024
    Abs DOI HTML

    Intensity-to-polarization leakage caused by calibration errors between detectors can be a significant systematic uncertainty for CMB polarization experiments. We describe the design and performance of a gain calibration system for the POLARBEAR-2a receiver in the Simons Array, using a chopped high-temperature ceramic heater as a blackbody source to calibrate detector gain responsivities before and after science observations.

  4. JLTP
    Deployment of POLARBEAR-2b
    M. Russell, K. Sakaguri, L. N. Lowry, and 14 more authors
    Journal of Low Temperature Physics, 2024
    Abs DOI HTML

    POLARBEAR-2b (PB-2b) is the second receiver in the Simons Array, a CMB polarization experiment located in the Atacama Desert at 5.2 km altitude. PB-2b contains approximately 7500 detectors operating in two frequency bands at 90 and 150 GHz with arcminute resolution, and its polarization is modulated by a cryogenic continuously rotating half-wave plate. We report on the installation, initial readout commissioning, and early characterization of PB-2b.

2023

  1. arXiv
    Predictive Power of Daily Viscacha and Vicuña Sightings on Simons Array Site Work Results
    P. Siritanasak, I. Birdwell, L. Lowry, and 2 more authors
    arXiv preprint, 2023
    Abs arXiv HTML

    We examine whether the daily number of viscacha and vicuña sightings at the Simons Array site in the Atacama Desert, Chile can predict operational work outcomes. Over 31 observation days, we find no statistically significant correlation between animal sightings and site work results, providing a lighthearted but rigorous statistical analysis of a site tradition.

  2. PRD
    Constraints on Axion-Like Polarization Oscillations in the Cosmic Microwave Background with POLARBEAR
    S. Adachi, T. Adkins, K. Arnold, and 28 more authors
    Physical Review D, 2023
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    Ultralight axion-like particles (ALPs) can couple to photons and induce a time-oscillating birefringence in the CMB polarization at a frequency equal to the ALP mass. We search for this signal in POLARBEAR data and report no detection, setting upper limits on the ALP-photon coupling constant as a function of ALP mass in a mass range inaccessible to other probes.

2022

  1. ApJ
    Improved Upper Limit on Degree-Scale CMB B-Mode Polarization Power from the 670 Square-Degree POLARBEAR Survey
    S. Adachi, T. Adkins, M. A. O. Aguilar Faúndez, and 81 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2022
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present an improved measurement of the degree-scale CMB B-mode angular power spectrum from 670 square degrees of POLARBEAR data at 150 GHz. By introducing an angle-correction algorithm the data volume is increased by a factor of 1.8. The measured spectrum is consistent with predictions from the Lambda CDM model with Galactic dust foregrounds, and we report improved upper limits on the tensor-to-scalar ratio.

  1. ApJ
    A Measurement of the CMB E-Mode Angular Power Spectrum at Subdegree Scales from 670 Square Degrees of POLARBEAR Data
    S. Adachi, M. A. O. Aguilar Faúndez, K. Arnold, and 83 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We report a measurement of the CMB E-mode polarization angular power spectrum at 150 GHz using POLARBEAR data spanning 2014–2016 across 670 square degrees at 32 \muK-arcmin noise. The EE power spectrum is measured across multiple acoustic peaks and is consistent with the standard \LambdaCDM cosmological prediction, with a probability-to-exceed of 0.38.

  2. ApJ
    Measurement of the Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization Lensing Power Spectrum from Two Years of POLARBEAR Data
    M. Aguilar Faúndez, K. Arnold, C. Baccigalupi, and 82 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present a measurement of the CMB polarization lensing power spectrum using two years of POLARBEAR observations from 2012 to 2014. We reject the no-lensing hypothesis at a confidence level of 10.9 sigma and report a lensing amplitude of A_L = 1.33^+0.46_-0.43(stat+sys), consistent with the \LambdaCDM expectation of A_L = 1.

  3. ApJ
    A Measurement of the Degree-Scale CMB B-Mode Angular Power Spectrum with POLARBEAR
    S. Adachi, M. A. O. Aguilar Faúndez, K. Arnold, and 83 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present a measurement of the CMB B-mode polarization power spectrum at degree scales with POLARBEAR. Observations at 150 GHz from 2014 to 2016 achieved a polarization map noise of 32 \muK-arcmin with sensitivity optimized around multipole \ell ≈90, where inflationary gravitational wave signals are predicted to be strongest. The measured spectrum is consistent with \LambdaCDM lensing and Galactic dust foreground models.

  4. PRL
    Internal Delensing of Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization B-Modes with the POLARBEAR Experiment
    S. Adachi, M. A. O. Aguilar Faúndez, Y. Akiba, and 82 more authors
    Physical Review Letters, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We demonstrate internal delensing of CMB polarization B-modes using only POLARBEAR CMB polarization data at subdegree scales, achieving greater than 5-sigma significance. A 14% reduction in B-mode power variance is achieved through internal delensing, improving to 2% with an iterative maximum a posteriori approach. This work demonstrates the capability of internal delensing for improving constraints on inflationary models.

    T. Namikawa, Y. Chinone, H. Miyatake, and 83 more authors

  4. ApJ
    Measurements of Tropospheric Ice Clouds with a Ground-Based CMB Polarization Experiment, POLARBEAR
    S. Takakura, M. A. O. Aguilar-Faúndez, Y. Akiba, and 83 more authors

  2. JCAP

    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

  3. A&A

    D. Poletti, G. Fabbian, M. Le Jeune, and 76 more authors
    Astronomy & Astrophysics, 2017
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We address filtering methods for CMB time-ordered data and construct a general filtering operator that can unambiguously remove any set of unwanted modes.   2. JLTP
    The Broadband Anti-Reflection Coated Extended Hemispherical Silicon Lenses for POLARBEAR-2 Experiment
    P. Siritanasak, P. Ade, Y. Akiba, and 70 more authors
    Journal of Low Temperature Physics, 2016
    Abs DOI HTML

    We present the design and fabrication of broadband anti-reflection (AR) coated extended hemispherical silicon lenses for the POLARBEAR-2 experiment. Each of the 1897 dual-polarization sinuous antenna-coupled pixels in the PB-2 receiver uses a directly contacting silicon lenslet, requiring broad-band AR coatings across 95–150 GHz.   3. JLTP
