Praween Siritanasak (Thai: ประวีณ สิริธนศักดิ์) was born and raised in Bangkok, Thailand. He earned his bachelor’s degree in Physics (First-class honors) from the Faculty of Science, Mahidol University, Thailand in 2009. In the same year, he received a fully-funded Royal Thai Government scholarship to pursue a doctoral degree in physics and astrophysics. He completed his Ph.D. from the Department of Physics at the University of California, San Diego in June 2018. From 2018–2020, he worked as a postdoctoral fellow in experimental cosmology at the Center for Astrophysics and Space Sciences (CASS) at UC San Diego under the supervision of Professor Brian Keating. He is currently working as a researcher at the National Astronomical Research Institute of Thailand (NARIT), Chiangmai, Thailand.

His research spans two fronts: CMB polarization through the POLARBEAR/Simons Array experiment in the Atacama Desert, Chile — building and testing ultra-sensitive detectors to hunt for primordial B-mode signals from the early Universe — and the search for dark photons with the Siam Dark Photon Consortium, chasing hints of hidden physics beyond the Standard Model.

Outside of research, Praween is an avid runner who has completed both ultra marathons and full marathons, with an ambition to earn the Abbott World Marathon Majors Six Star Medal. At heart, he is a nature lover who thrives on exploration — whether diving beneath the ocean, hiking up mountain trails, wandering new places, capturing the world through a lens, or simply lying back and losing himself in the night sky.

selected publications

  1. arXiv
    Predictive Power of Daily Viscacha and Vicuña Sightings on Simons Array Site Work Results
    P. Siritanasak, I. Birdwell, L. Lowry, and 2 more authors
    arXiv preprint, 2023
    Abs arXiv HTML

    We examine whether the daily number of viscacha and vicuña sightings at the Simons Array site in the Atacama Desert, Chile can predict operational work outcomes. Over 31 observation days, we find no statistically significant correlation between animal sightings and site work results, providing a lighthearted but rigorous statistical analysis of a site tradition.

  2. ApJ
    Measurement of the Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization Lensing Power Spectrum from Two Years of POLARBEAR Data
    M. Aguilar Faúndez, K. Arnold, C. Baccigalupi, and 82 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present a measurement of the CMB polarization lensing power spectrum using two years of POLARBEAR observations from 2012 to 2014. We reject the no-lensing hypothesis at a confidence level of 10.9 sigma and report a lensing amplitude of A_L = 1.33^+0.46_-0.43(stat+sys), consistent with the \LambdaCDM expectation of A_L = 1.

  3. ApJ
    A Measurement of the Degree-Scale CMB B-Mode Angular Power Spectrum with POLARBEAR
    S. Adachi, M. A. O. Aguilar Faúndez, K. Arnold, and 83 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present a measurement of the CMB B-mode polarization power spectrum at degree scales with POLARBEAR. Observations at 150 GHz from 2014 to 2016 achieved a polarization map noise of 32 \muK-arcmin with sensitivity optimized around multipole \ell ≈90, where inflationary gravitational wave signals are predicted to be strongest. The measured spectrum is consistent with \LambdaCDM lensing and Galactic dust foreground models.

  4. PRL
    Internal Delensing of Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization B-Modes with the POLARBEAR Experiment
    S. Adachi, M. A. O. Aguilar Faúndez, Y. Akiba, and 82 more authors
    Physical Review Letters, 2020
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We demonstrate internal delensing of CMB polarization B-modes using only POLARBEAR CMB polarization data at subdegree scales, achieving greater than 5-sigma significance. A 14% reduction in B-mode power variance is achieved through internal delensing, improving to 2% with an iterative maximum a posteriori approach. This work demonstrates the capability of internal delensing for improving constraints on inflationary models.

  5. ApJ
    A Measurement of the Cosmic Microwave Background B-Mode Polarization Power Spectrum at Sub-Degree Scales from 2 Years of POLARBEAR Data
    The POLARBEAR Collaboration, P. A. R. Ade, M. Aguilar, and 91 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2017
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present improved CMB B-mode polarization power spectrum measurements at 150 GHz combining data from two POLARBEAR observation seasons. By incorporating 2013–2014 data with re-analyzed 2012–2013 observations, we achieve a twofold reduction in band-power uncertainties across angular multipoles 500–2100, constraining the lensing B-mode amplitude to A_\mathrmlens = 1.06 \pm 0.24\mathrm(stat) \pm 0.11\mathrm(sys).

  6. JLTP
    The Broadband Anti-Reflection Coated Extended Hemispherical Silicon Lenses for POLARBEAR-2 Experiment
    P. Siritanasak, P. Ade, Y. Akiba, and 70 more authors
    Journal of Low Temperature Physics, 2016
    Abs DOI HTML

    We present the design and fabrication of broadband anti-reflection (AR) coated extended hemispherical silicon lenses for the POLARBEAR-2 experiment. Each of the 1897 dual-polarization sinuous antenna-coupled pixels in the PB-2 receiver uses a directly contacting silicon lenslet, requiring broad-band AR coatings across 95–150 GHz. We developed a two-layer epoxy-based AR coating using Stycast 1090 and Stycast 2850FT and characterize its optical performance.

  7. ApJ
    A Measurement of the Cosmic Microwave Background B-Mode Polarization Power Spectrum at Sub-Degree Scales with POLARBEAR
    The POLARBEAR Collaboration, P. A. R. Ade, Y. Akiba, and 64 more authors
    The Astrophysical Journal, 2014
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We report a measurement of the B-mode polarization power spectrum of the CMB with POLARBEAR at 150 GHz. Observations over approximately 25 deg^2 across three independent fields at 500 < \ell < 2100 provide evidence for a non-zero B-mode signal at 2.3σsignificance. The best-fit lensing amplitude is A_\mathrmlens = 1.12 \pm 0.61\mathrm(stat)^+0.04_-0.12\mathrm(sys) \pm 0.07\mathrm(mul).

  8. PRL
    Measurement of the Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization Lensing Power Spectrum with the POLARBEAR Experiment
    The POLARBEAR Collaboration, P. A. R. Ade, Y. Akiba, and 64 more authors
    Physical Review Letters, 2014
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We present the first direct measurement of the CMB polarization lensing power spectrum with POLARBEAR using four-point correlations of E- and B-mode polarization data across approximately 30 square degrees of sky, providing direct evidence for gravitational lensing of the CMB polarization.

  9. PRL
    Evidence for Gravitational Lensing of the Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization from Cross-Correlation with the Cosmic Infrared Background
    The POLARBEAR Collaboration, P. A. R. Ade, Y. Akiba, and 64 more authors
    Physical Review Letters, 2014
    Abs DOI arXiv HTML

    We reconstruct gravitational lensing signals from CMB polarization measurements from POLARBEAR and cross-correlate them with infrared background maps from the Herschel satellite. We detect evidence for gravitational lensing of the CMB polarization at 4.0σstatistical significance.
